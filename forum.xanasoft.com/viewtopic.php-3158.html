Alternatively, you can try changing the MAC address of your network adapter. I haven't tried this myself, but I've heard that it can work. Just make sure to do it properly so you don't mess up your computer's settings. As for changing the network adapter itself, I'm not sure if that would work. It's worth a shot, but it might be more complicated than just assigning different IP addresses. Oh, and speaking of routers, have you tried logging in to your TP-Link router settings? It might be worth checking out to see if there's anything you can do to fix your IP address conflict.
