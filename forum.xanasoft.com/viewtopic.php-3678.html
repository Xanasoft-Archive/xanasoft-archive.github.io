Hysolate still runs an entire operating system and (I assume) just hides the OS from the user. However, any type of virtual machine will have overhead. The whole point of Hysolate is it automates the virtual machine process to make it more user friendly. However, Sandboxie also has less system requirements than Hysolate (and VMs in general), including not requiring turning on virtualization in the BIOS, not requiring an SSD with 10 GB of free space (used to store the virtualized OS; SSD requirement is specific to Hysolate), and it works on Windows 10 Home (Hysolate Free requires at least Win10 Pro).
